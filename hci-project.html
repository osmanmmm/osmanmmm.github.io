Project Description

CampusCare is a centralized platform that consolidates mental health resources for Carleton students. It simplifies access to wellness tools, reduces misinformation, and provides a peer support space. The platform integrates events, therapist information, and student groups to improve mental well-being at Carleton College.

Our Final Three Requirements

  1. Counseling Options & Scheduling: provides students with counseling services and step-by-step scheduling instructions.
  2. Common Q&As & Misconceptions: Clarify misunderstandings through student bogs about mental health resources and address frequently asked questions.
  3. Resource Directory & Wellness Events: Centralize all mental health resources, including a clear calendar of upcoming events and workshops.

How Our Mid-Fidelity Prototype Addresses These Requirements

  • Counseling Options & Scheduling: We created a scheduling page with dropdown menus for appointment type, medical professional, and location. Users can see all available counseling services (SHAC, TimelyCare, peer counseling) and easily book a session.
  • Common Q&As & Misconceptions: Our prototype includes a FAQ section with items for each question. This helps dispel misinformation about mental health resources, transportation, and appointment costs.
  • Resource Directory & Wellness Events: We implemented a Resources page featuring multiple filter options (counseling, events, crisis help) and a separate calendar page listing upcoming wellness workshops and mindfulness sessions.

UX Research Process

We conducted semi-structured interviews with Carleton students to understand their current pain points and gaps in mental health resources. We then used affinity diagramming to group recurring themes, such as difficulty scheduling counseling, confusion around available services, and a lack of centralized resource listings.

Analysis Process

Using axial coding, we distilled these themes into our three core requirements: improving scheduling clarity, addressing FAQs & misconceptions, and centralizing resources. This process guided our mid-fidelity prototype design to directly target the most critical user needs.
